Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to the most common questions about our services. If you still need help, feel free to reach out we’re here for you.

01

Do I really need an app, or is a website enough?

A website and an app do different jobs. A website is for visitors who don’t know you yet. An app is for the customers who already do, and who you want to come back. If your business depends on repeat visits, bookings, or a direct relationship with your customers, an app pays for itself. If you mostly need to be found in Google search results, a website might be enough. We’ll tell you honestly which one you need on the first call.

02

How much does an app cost?

It depends on what the app does. A focused MVP for a startup typically lands between $20,000 and $60,000. A full production app for an established business is usually $60,000 to $150,000. Apps with complex backends, multiple user roles, or heavy AI features can go higher. We give you a written, fixed-scope quote before any work starts — not estimates that creep upward.

03

How long does it take?

A simple app: 8 to 12 weeks. A medium-complexity app: 3 to 6 months. A complex platform: 6 to 12 months. We’ll give you a realistic timeline on the discovery call.

04

Do I need iOS, Android, or both?

Most of our clients launch on both. Cross-platform tools like React Native and Flutter let us build one codebase that runs on both stores, which keeps cost reasonable. If your customers are mostly on iPhones (US-based, premium audiences) or mostly on Android (global, value-conscious audiences), starting with one is a smart way to validate before expanding.

05

Will I own the code?

Yes. Always. Every line of code, every design file, every credential — yours. We give you a complete handoff at launch and the right to take everything to another developer if you ever choose to.

06

What about updates and maintenance after launch?

Apps need updates. iOS and Android release new operating systems every year, and your app needs to keep up. We offer affordable monthly maintenance plans that cover OS updates, security patches, bug fixes, and small improvements. You can also bring us in just for major changes — no monthly commitment required.

07

I have an idea but I don't know if it'll work. Can you help me figure that out?

Yes. That’s actually what we love most. Our discovery process is designed exactly for this — we’ll help you pressure-test the idea, identify what to build first to validate it, and tell you honestly if we think it’ll work. Sometimes we tell people not to build the app they came in for. We’d rather lose a project than build something we know won’t succeed.

08

I already have an app that someone else built. Can you take it over?

Often, yes. We do code audits to see what we’re working with and tell you honestly whether it makes sense to continue, refactor, or rebuild. We’ll never tell you to rebuild for the sake of rebuilding.

09

I'm based in Dallas (or Houston, or anywhere in Texas) — does that change anything?

If you’re in Texas, we can do in-person meetings whenever they help — kickoff workshops in Dallas, working sessions in Houston, lunch in Austin. It’s part of why having a Texas headquarters matters for clients who want a local partner. If you’re outside Texas or outside the US, we work the way most modern teams do: video calls, shared docs, and weekly demo builds you can install on your phone. The outcomes are the same either way.

10

What if I want to add AI features?

We can. AI is a tool, not a product — we’ll add it where it solves a real user problem (smart search, personalized recommendations, document recognition, in-app assistants) and we’ll talk you out of it where it doesn’t. We won’t add AI for the sake of saying your app has AI.

11

How do I know you're a good fit?

The discovery call. It’s free, it’s an hour, and you’ll come away with a clearer picture of what to build and what it’ll cost — whether you hire us or not.
